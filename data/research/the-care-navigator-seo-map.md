# Care Navigator SEO and Competitor Map

Captured: 2026-06-02

## Official CHC Source Baseline

- NHS describes NHS Continuing Healthcare as a package of care arranged and funded solely by the NHS for adults with a primary health need.
- GOV.UK Checklist guidance states that the Checklist is a screening tool. A positive Checklist means a full assessment is needed; it does not automatically mean CHC eligibility.
- GOV.UK Decision Support Tool guidance supports consistent evidence-based recommendations and decisions on eligibility.

Primary sources:

- https://www.nhs.uk/social-care-and-support/money-work-and-benefits/nhs-continuing-healthcare/
- https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/nhs-continuing-healthcare-checklist/nhs-continuing-healthcare-checklist-guidance
- https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/nhs-continuing-healthcare-decision-support-tool

## Competitor Patterns

### ARROW CHC Consultants

Observed pattern:

- Exact service naming: free consultation, eligibility review, comprehensive support, reviews, appeals and retrospective funding.
- Strong process language: contact, build the case, prepare for the CHC meeting.
- SEO advantage: the page repeats `NHS Continuing Healthcare`, `CHC`, `eligibility review`, `appeals`, `assessment`, `review` and `medical reports`.

Implication:

- Care Navigator should use clear service names and show the preparation journey without promising success.

### Beacon CHC

Observed pattern:

- High trust because it is connected to NHS-funded free information and advice.
- Strong educational pages around appeals, Decision Support Tool, assessment and challenge routes.
- Uses free toolkit language and clear public guidance.

Implication:

- Care Navigator should build a resource library with plain-English guides and official-source accuracy.

### Oxmoor Care Consultancy

Observed pattern:

- Combines independent care advice, CHC support, care planning and professional audiences.
- Speaks directly to families, solicitors and financial advisers.
- Uses later-life care planning and capacity-adjacent language.

Implication:

- Care Navigator should not be CHC-only. It should visibly support self-funding, complex care planning and professional referrals.

### My Care Consultant

Observed pattern:

- Strong professional referral path for financial advisers and legal representatives.
- Talks about self-funding families, long-term care, affordability, stress and unsuitable decisions.
- Uses adviser resources and a care hub to capture professional SEO.

Implication:

- Add `For Professionals`, `Professional Referrals` and article tags for deputies, attorneys, solicitors and financial advisers.

### Nova Care Consultants

Observed pattern:

- Positions around CHC appeals, mental capacity assessments, Section 117 and named expert support.
- Uses hourly rates on some pages, but the Care Navigator plan intentionally avoids publishing prices.

Implication:

- Care Navigator should keep service pages price-free and encourage consultation-led scoping.

### Jane's Healthcare

Observed pattern:

- Founder-expert positioning around a nurse and CHC/case management experience.
- Strong personal expertise claim and direct CHC language.

Implication:

- Yvonne Brown’s founder-led experience should be above the fold and repeated in proof modules.

### Continuing Healthcare Expert

Observed pattern:

- Uses terms such as Checklist Advocacy, DST outcome scrutiny, reviews, resolutions and appeals.
- Emphasizes representation and independent support.

Implication:

- Service page titles should include `Checklist`, `Decision Support Tool`, `MDT`, `CHC review` and `appeal` through articles and FAQs.

## Blog Cluster Map

- NHS CHC: Checklist, full assessment, Decision Support Tool, ICB communication.
- Care funding: CHC, local authority, self-funding, reviews, provider questions.
- Self-funding: care planning risks, family capacity, provider review, when to seek regulated financial advice.
- CHC appeals: decision letters, disputed domains, evidence, calm written submissions.
- MDT meetings: preparation, notes, questions, family voice.
- Hospital discharge: discharge planning, reablement, assessment timing, care-package risks.
- Professional deputies: referral routes, care context, practical reports and family communication.

## Copy Rules

- Do not guarantee funding outcomes.
- Do not present guidance as legal, financial or clinical advice.
- Use official terms accurately: Checklist, Decision Support Tool, Multi-Disciplinary Team, Integrated Care Board.
- Lead with family anxiety and practical next steps, then explain evidence and process.
- Keep CTAs consistent: `Book a Free Initial Consultation`, `Call Now`, `Email Us`, `Send Professional Referral`.
